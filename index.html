Important Mathematical Facts

• Prime Number Distribution:
There is no known simple formula that generates all prime numbers. The function π(x), which counts primes less than x, is approximately x / ln(x), but the exact distribution remains unpredictable.

• Irrational Numbers:
Numbers like √2, π, and e cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers. Their decimal expansions are infinite and non-repeating.

• Non-Elementary Integrals:
Not all integrals have closed-form solutions. For example, ∫ e−x² dx cannot be expressed using elementary functions.

• Gödel’s Incompleteness Theorem:
In any sufficiently powerful formal system, there exist true mathematical statements that cannot be proven within the system itself.

Problem 3 (Calculus):

Problem 4 (Geometry):
